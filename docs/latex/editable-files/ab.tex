\section*{ABSTRACT} 

\parbox[1in]{2in}{ }

% Provide Your Abstract below. No editing above this line

Cold plasma, also known as non-thermal plasma, has gained significant attention for its applications in biomedical treatment, surface modification, sterilization, and material processing. 
This project focuses on the design and development of a Cold Plasma Generator using a high-voltage pulsed power supply. 
The system is implemented using an STM32G431 microcontroller to generate PWM signals, which drive an H-bridge inverter composed of IRFP460 MOSFETs through a UCC21330 isolated gate driver. 
The H-bridge output excites a high-voltage transformer, and the stepped-up AC is further boosted using a Cockcroft-Walton voltage multiplier. 
The resulting high-voltage pulses are applied to an argon gas flow to produce a visible cold plasma jet at atmospheric pressure. 
The proposed system demonstrates a low-cost and efficient method for generating cold plasma suitable for experimental and industrial research applications.
